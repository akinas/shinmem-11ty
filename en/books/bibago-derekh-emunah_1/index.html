The Path of Faith

Rabbi Abraham ben Shem Tov Bibago

Book One

One of the last great works of Spanish Jewry before the Expulsion, The Path of Faith (Derekh Emunah) by Rabbi Abraham ben Shem Tov Bibago stands as a unique fusion of philosophy, Jewish tradition, and mysticism. Written in the twilight of medieval Spanish Judaism, this philosophical homily blends deep intellectual inquiry with traditional sources and esoteric thought, offering a rare window into the spiritual world of 15th-century Jewish thinkers.

Divided into three major sections, the work explores fundamental theological and philosophical issues. The first part explores divine action, knowledge, and providence; the second examines the nature of the intellect and the intricate relationship between faith and reason; the third presents a comprehensive discussion of the principles of Jewish belief, particularly as articulated by Maimonides.

Despite its historical and intellectual importance, The Path of Faith has never received a full critical edition – until now. This edition presents a carefully edited version of the first part of the work, based on all extant manuscripts and the original printed edition. It provides a reliable and authoritative text for scholars, students, and anyone interested in Jewish philosophy and the legacy of Sephardic thought. This volume marks the first step in restoring Rabbi Bibago’s voice to its rightful place in the history of Jewish ideas.
